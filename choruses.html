Ain't No Ash Will Burn

Love is a precious thing, I'm told; Burns just like West Virginia coal. But when the fire dies down, it's cold: There ain't no ash will burn.

Alice and Jessie

Alice was married in Baltimore, In a long dress of satin and pearls; Jessie she ran with a dark ramblin' man, And he carried her over the world.

All the Salt

All the salt in the sea cannot preserve me; All the heat in the fire cannot bring my heart's desire; And all the birds in the sky cannot teach me to fly; But the love in my soul can keep me whole.

Anderson's Coast

Old Bass Strait roars like some great millrace, And where are you, my Annie? And the same moon shines on this lonely place, As shone one day on my Annie's face.

O Annie, dear, don't wait for me, I fear I shall not return to thee. There's nought to do but endure my fate, And watch the moon, the lonely moon Light the breakers on wild Bass Strait.

Annan Water

Oh woe betide you, Annan Water. By night you are a gloomy river, And over you I'll build a bridge That never more true love may sever.

Archimedes (Use the Lever)

The lever, boys, the lever, Oh, the lever, boys, the lever! Not the pulley, the screw, Nor the inclined plane. Boys, it's time to use the lever!

Beer That Tastes Like Beer

Here's to beer that tastes like beer, An amber glass of wholesome cheer, A noble brew that has no peer: Beer that tastes like beer!

Bold Riley

Oh the rain it raineth all day long, Bold Riley O, Bold Riley... And the northern wind, it blows so strong, Bold Riley O has gone away.

Goodbye my sweetheart, goodbye my dear O, Bold Riley O, Bold Riley; Goodbye my darlin', goodbye my dear O, Bold Riley O has gone away.

Bonnet and Shawl

And I'll show you the sun 'cross the fields in the morn, I'll fetch thee a bonnet and deck it with corn. I'll buy thee a shawl thread with ribbons of blue To show you the measure I'd trouble for you.

Bonny Barque the Bergen

Sleep, why d'you wake me with these dreams you bring? Dreams came to me where I lay. And deep the melody the wild waves sing, And my love is far, far away.

Pity the hearts the wild waves part; My love sails the bonny barque, the Bergen.

Cairo Town (The Roustabout Song)

I'm rolling, flowing, around these hills; I must take my rest, but this river never will. Rolling, flowing, to Cairo Town, Just give me time to lay me down.

The Chimera

I am the chimera, it's fire I breathe; I do not dissemble, I do not deceive; All that I am is all that you see; "Unnatural" means nothing to me.

Cool of the Day

Now is the cool of the day (2x) Oh, this earth is a garden The garden of my Lord And He walks in His garden In the cool of the day.

Country Life (I Like to Rise)

I like to rise when the sun she rises Early in the morning; I like to hear them small birds singing Merrily upon their laylum; And hurrah for the life of a country boy, And to ramble in the new-mown hay.

Cows Can't Swim

Oh, the cow and the grain and the goat and the boat, Oh how would I get them across that great moat? The grain felt no pain and the goat he might float, but cows can't swim!

Cremation of Sam McGee

There are strange things done in the midnight sun

By the men who moil for gold;

The Arctic trails have their secret tales

That would make your blood run cold;

The Northern Lights have seen queer sights,

But the queerest they ever did see

Was that night on the marge of Lake Lebarge

I cremated Sam McGee.

Dark as a Dungeon

It's dark as a dungeon and damp as the dew, Where dangers are double and pleasures are few; Where the rain never falls and the sun never shines, It's dark as a dungeon way down in the mines.

Davy Cross

How we knew his bright blue eyes How we knew his golden hair; And the gansey that his mother made was fine beyond compare.

Do You Love an Apple (Still I Love Him)

Do you love an apple? Do you love a pear? Do you love a laddie with curly brown hair? Ah, but still I love him, I can't deny him; I'll be with him wherever he goes.

Doin' My Best

Doin' my best, doin' my best, Yes I always try to do my best. Though many songs are gayer, prouder, Many voices sweeter, louder, Still, I do my best!

Final Trawl

Now it's three long years since we made her pay, Sing haul away, my laddie-o, And the owners say that she's had her day, And sing haul away, my laddie-o.

Follow the Heron

By night and day we'll sport and we'll play And delight as the dawn dances over the bay. Sleep blows the breath of the morning away And we follow the heron home.

Frozen in Frobisher Bay

Cold is the Arctic Sea, Far are your arms from me, Long will this winter be Frozen in Frobisher Bay (2x)

Getting Dark Again

Getting dark again (2x) For the second time since we got up It's getting dark again.

The Goodnight Song (Goodnight My Friends)

So goodnight my friends, as the dawn comes pale, And the eastern wind brings the threat of gale. Keep a hold on hope through the darkest vale, And we'll meet further on down the road.

Haul Away Joe

When I was just a little thing, my mama often told me Away, haul away, we'll haul away, Joe! That if I were to kiss them all, I'd better kiss them boldly. Away, haul away, we'll haul away, Joe!

Away (HEY!), haul away, we'll haul away together, Away, haul away, we'll haul away, Joe! Away (HEY!), haul away, we'll haul for better weather, Away, haul away, we'll haul away, Joe!

Here Is My Home

Good from whom we now must part, Where are we bound? Your hands and voices lift my heart, Here is my home.

Come darkness, come light, Where are we bound? Come morning, come night, Here is my home.

Hey Rain (The Innisfail Song)

Hey rain, hey rain, hey rain, Rain coming down on the cane, On the roofs of the town.

I Can Hew

I can hew, boys, I can hack it out, I can hew the coal, I can dance and shout. I can hew, boys, coal that's black and fine; I'm a collier lad workin' down the mine.

It's a Pleasure to Know You

It's a pleasure to know you, a pleasure to see you smile, A comfort to know we'll share the road awhile. Pleasure is fleeting, and comforts are far between; It's a pleasure to know you, and the comfort you bring.

Johnny Come Down to Hilo

Never seen the like since I been born, An Arkansas farmer with his sea boots on. Johnny come down to Hilo, poor old man.

Wake her, shake her, Shake that gal with the blue dress on. Johnny come down to Hilo, poor old man.

Katy Cruel

When I first came to town They called me the roving jewel. Now they've changed their tune, They call me Katy Cruel. Oh, the diddle-i-di-ay, o the diddle-i-di-do-day.

O that I was what I would be, Than should I be where I am not. (Clap) Here am I where I must be, Where I would be I cannot Go, the diddle-i-di-ay, o the diddle-i-di-do-day.

Lad I Love Dearly

When I look to yon high hills, my laddie's not there. When I look to yon high hills, it makes my heart sair. When I look to yon high hills, a tear fills my eye; For the lad I love dearly lies a distance fae me.

The Last Leviathan

My soul has been torn from me, and I am bleeding. My heart it has been rent, and I am crying. As the beauty around me fails, and I am screaming, I am the last of the great whales, and I am dying.

Leave Her Johnny

I thought I heard the old man say, Leave her, Johnny, leave her You can go ashore and take your pay / Tomorrow ye will get your pay! And it's time for us to leave her.

Leave her, Johnny, leave her! Oh, leave her, Johnny, leave her; For the voyage is done and the winds don't blow And it's time for us to leave her!

Let Union Be in All Our Hearts

Let union be in all our hearts, Let all our hearts be joined as one. We'll end the day as we begun, We'll end it all in pleasure.

Right-folla-rolla-rolla, too-ra-lie-doe (x3) While we are together.

Linnaeus (One Species Are We)

Linnaeus! Linnaeus! Here's to your hierarchy; And let it not betray us! One species are we!

Lovely Agnes

Oh Agnes, won't you go with me? We'll be married in style, And we'll cross Lake Michigan, so blue and so wild; We'll cross over Lake Michigan till we come to the shore, And our orchards will blossom for our babies as they're born.

Maid of Amsterdam (A-Rovin')

In Amsterdam there lived a maid, Mark well what I do say! In Amsterdam there lived a maid And she was mistress of her trade. I'll go no more a-rovin' with you, fair maid!

A-rovin', a-rovin', since rovin's been my ru-i-in, I'll go no more a-rovin' with you, fair maid!

Mama (I'm Grown Up, Mama)

Well I'm grown up, Mama, doin' right by you, Living hard like you taught me to do. I've got no regrets, only unpaid debts For all the kindness from people like you.

Mary Had a Little Lamb

Hurrah for Mary! Hurrah for the lamb! Hurrah for the Union Boys who did not give a damn! And everywhere that Mary went the lamb was sure to go, Shouting the battle cry of freedom!

Mingulay Boat Song

Heel yo ho, boys; let her go, boys; Bring her head round, into the weather. Heel you ho, boys, let her go, boys, Sailing homeward to Mingulay.

Old Jacky Frost

Summer's lost and the winter's come; Don't let him in, no way sir. Days are short and the nights are long; Don't let him in, no way sir. Hear him knocking, don't complain, See his star on the windowpane. Comes the winter, comes what may, Don't let him in, no way sir.

Old Jacky Frost is knockin' at the door,

Don't let him in, no way sir.

Old Jacky Frost, been here before,

Don't let him in, no way sir.

Whether you're rich or whether you're poor,
He'll come knockin' all around your door.
Give him a chance, he'll be in for sure,

Don't let him in, no way sir.

Old Zeb

Rosie, get my Sunday shoes, Gertie get my walking cane; We'll take another walk to see old Alice sail again.

Raise Your Voice

So raise your voice with mine (2x) For now's the perfect time to join the song.

Rollin' Down the River

I once was a rigger and I worked like hell, Rollin' up, rollin' down, And now I'm a sailor with the OCL. And go rollin' down the river.

Rollin' up, rollin' down, We'll all get drunk in Tilbury town. In twenty-four hours we'll turn around And go rollin' down the river.

Row On

Row on, row on, another day May shine with brighter light. Ply, ply the oars and pull away; There's dawn beyond the night.

Santa Georgia

...In the land of Santa Georgia where the banks of burdocks grow.

Where old panes are broken and a thousand tongues are spoken, And new saints awaken where the banks of burdocks grow; So farewell cold winter, we will all shine out together In the land of Santa Georgia where the banks of burdocks grow.

Sarah, Won't You Come Out Tonight

Sarah, Sarah, won't you come out tonight? Sarah, Sarah, the moon is shining bright. Get your hat and jacket on, And tell your mother you won't be long, And I'll be waiting for you 'round the corner.

Sea Creature of Greenland

It was a most unusual sight, Wondrous and wiggling and wrinkled and white. Its head as high as the ship, Its tail behind so long and thick: The sea creature of Greenland, Most dreadful and monstrously big.

Sing, John Ball

Sing, John Ball, and tell it to them all! Long live the day that is dawning! I'll crow like a cock, I'll carol like a lark, In the light that is coming in the morning!

Songs Stay Sung

And all we do may be undone, But love stays loved and songs stay sung, Love stays loved and songs stay sung.

South Australia

In South Australia I was born! Heave away! Haul away! In South Australia round Cape Horn! We're bound for South Australia!

Heave away, you rolling kings, Heave away! Haul away! Haul away, you'll hear me sing We're bound for South Australia!

Spider in the Bath

Here I am—is anyone about? I'm down beside the plug-hole and I can't get out, and I've been here an hour and a half. Can't anybody help a little spider in the bath?

Stand Together (Grow Our Village Stronger)

When we all [stand] together,

we grow our village stronger.

We are stronger than we realize,

we are stronger than we know;

So [stand] together, love each other,

share in joys and sorrows

And we'll grow our village stronger, this we know.

Sweet Mary Starbuck

Sweet Mary Starbuck from Nantucket town, If she were the sea I would willingly drown; And her voice like a bell on a clear winter's day Sets my heart a-ringing and winging away.

Tide and the River Rising (Oars and Row)

Come on, get your oars and row, my darling Come on, get your oars and row; We've got tide and the river rising, Come on, get your oars and row.

Turning Towards the Morning

Oh my Joanie don't you know That the stars are swinging slow, And the seas are rolling easy As they did so long ago? And if I had a thing to give you, I would tell you one more time That the world is always turning toward the morning.

Union Maid

Oh, you can't scare me, I'm sticking to the union (3x) Oh, you can't scare me, I'm sticking to the union, I'm sticking to the union 'til the day I die.

Valparaiso in a Rowboat

Pull your oars! (Pull your oars) We're bound away! (We're bound away!) With a thousand quarts of whisky bound for Valparaiso Bay, And we have no intention for to see the U.S.A.; We're bound for Valparaiso in a rowboat.

The Vexings (Have You Got a Girlfriend Yet?)

..."Oh dear, oh dear, my dearest dear,

have you got a girlfriend yet?"

No hi's, hellos, or how've you been's, Just the vexings you beget, For it's every damn time you see my face, you'll say: "Have you got a girlfriend yet?"

We Are Here and We Are Rising

We are here and we are [rising],
We are here and we are strong,
We are here for all our neighbors

and the ones already gone.

We are here and we are [rising], We are here and we are strong, We are here and we are [rising], We are [rising], we hold on.

When We Sing Together

When we sing together,

it's like we were never alone.

We want to forever believe

our music will ring where green things grow.

We know it'll fade, so we

sing ever stronger, fill up our hearts with home;

When we sing together,

it's like we were never alone.

Who Will Watch the Home Place

Who will watch the home place? Who will tend my heart's dear space? Who will fill my empty place, When I am gone from here?

Wild Mountain Thyme

Will you go, lassie, will you go? And we'll all go together To pull wild mountain thyme All around the blooming heather, Will you go, lassie, go?

Wild Rover

And it's no, nay, never, (clap 4x) No, nay never no more Will I play the wild rover, No, never, no more.

Witch Hazel

I am lookin' at witch hazel bloomin' in a garden, Bright yellow flowers in the middle of wintertime; And I tell my heart be strong like the witch hazel flower, And you will not be injured by this dark and troubled time.
