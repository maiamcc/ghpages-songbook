Raise Your Voice

Jess Arrowsmith

Once there was a man and he had no song.
He felt his head was empty and his voice was wrong.
And though he often tried, his songs were trapped inside;
He couldn't find the place where they belong

But raise your voice with mine (2x) For now's the perfect time to join the song.

Once there was a woman and her songs were loud.
She sang them in the shower and she sang them proud!
But when her friends were near, her voice, it shrank with fear,
And left her shaking mute before the crowd.

Now if you want to sing but don't know how or why,
Don't listen to the ones who say you should not try!
There's bound to be a way that you've not tried before today
To free your voice to soar beneath the sky.

Once there was a singer and they made their choice
To leave their doubts behind them, and to find their voice.
The journey that they're on is to learn just one more song,
And sing it out and make their friends rejoice.

Notes: Jess Arrowsmith writes: "for everybody who has ever been told to mime at the back of the choir, or finds singing in front of others mind-blankingly terrifying." Repeated chorus line comes from Emma.
