Archimedes (Use the Lever)

Nat Case

Archimedes was a fine old man,
Of that I'm a believer.
He invented hair oil and the frying pan,
And he taught us 'bout the lever.

The lever, boys, the lever, Oh, the lever, boys, the lever! Not the pulley, the screw, Nor the inclined plane. Boys, it's time to use the lever!

Oh the inclined plane, it may launch our ship,
And the screw, it may well sink her.
And the pulleys we pull in the rigging all day,
But what about the lever?

When the grog is brought up on deck,
Of thirst, it's a reliever.
And if the bung won't leave the bunghole, then
It's time to use the lever!

Archimedes, he is dead and gone—
May God be his receiver.
And we'll dig his grave with a silver spade
Which, in fact, is just a lever!
