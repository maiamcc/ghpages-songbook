Never seen the like since I been born,
An Arkansas farmer with his sea boots on.

Johnny come down to Hilo, poor old man.
Wake her, shake her, Shake that gal with the blue dress on. Johnny come down to Hilo, poor old man.

Who's been here since I been gone,
That pretty little gal with the blue dress on.

Johnny come down...

She's a downeast gal with a downeast style,
For a dollar a time and it's all worthwhile.

Downeast gals they dress so fine,
They ain't got Jesus on their mind.

My wife she died in Tennessee,
And they sent her jawbone back to me.

I hung that jawbone on the fence
And I ain't heard nothing but the jawbone since.

So hand me down my riding cane
I'm off to see Ms. Sarah Jane.

I shook her up, I shook her down,
I shook her 'round and 'round the town.

I called at night, I called at day,
I called before I sailed away.

Notes: For easier range, sing verse/chorus going down to tonic instead of 5th.

High tonic "OH wake her" can be reserved for the leader, leave it out the first few times of the chorus so the room doesn't interalize it.

Hilo -- port in Hawaii, in Peru? Substitute for a 'do' / 'jamboree'? Used as a verb—to 'hilo' somebody/smth?
