Roll the Old Chariot Along (A Drop of Nelson's Blood)

trad.

Oh, we’d be alright if the wind was in our sails (3x)

And we’ll all hang on behind.
(And) We’ll roll the old chariot along (3x) And we’ll all hang on behind.

We’d be alright if we make it around the horn.

Well, a nice watch below wouldn’t do us any harm.

Well, a drop of Nelson’s blood wouldn’t do us any harm.

Well, a night on the town wouldn’t do us any harm.

Notes: Following his victory and death at the Battle of Trafalgar, Nelson’s body was (allegedly) preserved in a cask of brandy or rum for transport back to England. ‘Nelson’s blood’ became a nickname for rum, but it can also mean Nelson’s spirit or bravery.

Presumably a shanty; the chorus comes from a 19th cen. Salvation Army hymn. May also have spiritual/gospel origins??
