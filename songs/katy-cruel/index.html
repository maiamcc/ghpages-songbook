When I first came to town
They called me the roving jewel.
Now they've changed their tune,
They call me Katy Cruel.

Oh, the diddle-i-di-ay, o the diddle-i-di-do-day.
O that I was what I would be, Than should I be where I am not. (Clap) Here am I where I must be, Where I would be I cannot Go, the diddle-i-di-ay, o the diddle-i-di-do-day.

Through the woods I go,
And through the boggy mire,
Straightway down the road
And to my heart's desire.

Eyes as black as coal,
Lips as bright as cherry,
And 'tis her delight
To make the young girls merry.
