Getting Dark Again

Buddy MacDonald

There's a piper in the corner,
There's a dancer on the floor;
Friends around the table,
There's one who calls for more;
And there's Gaelic in the fiddle
Like the Gaelic in the Glen;
And the songs take on new meaning, boys,
It's getting dark again.

Getting dark again (2x) For the second time since we got up It's getting dark again

There was tea and tunes this morning—
Or was it yesterday?
When friends and fiddles gathered
Where friends and fiddles played.
Well we had so many good times good times,
And do you remember when...
But that was jigs and reels ago,
It's getting dark again.

If the ev'ryday seems dimmer now you know what can be done
With the fellowship of music and the fantasy of fun;
Well we'd like to say we're sorry,
Shed a tear for you, but then:
We haven't got the time right now:
It's getting dark again.

(May repeat first verse.)

Notes: Buddy MacDonald is a Cape Breton singer/songwriter. Song was first recorded(?) in 1996. About staying up all night and the next day in the magic of dance camp etc.

Learned from Christa Burch at American Week 2023; last verse is my modification.
