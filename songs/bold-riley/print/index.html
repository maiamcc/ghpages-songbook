Bold Riley

trad.

Oh the rain it raineth all day long,

Bold Riley O, Bold Riley...

And the northern wind, it blows so strong,

Bold Riley O has gone away.
Goodbye my sweetheart, goodbye my dear O, Bold Riley O, Bold Riley; Goodbye my darlin', goodbye my dear O, Bold Riley O has gone away.

Oh the anchor's weighed and the sails are set,

Bold Riley O...

And them Liverpool lasses we'll ne'er forget.

Bold Riley O...

Well come on Mary, don't look glum
Come White-Stocking Day you'll be drinkin' rum.

We're outward bound for the Bengal Bay,
Keep rowing boys, it's a bloody long way

Notes: A.L. Lloyd 1962 liner notes: "Shellbacks manning the windjammers of the West Indies trade brought back to Liverpool and Bristol more than sugar, bananas and rum; they also brought back many songs. Some of these they kept to themselves, some they handed on to vessels sailing in other waters. Thus the fine hexatonic tune of Bold Riley O, which started life as a Tobago reel, was sung at the halyards of many an East Indiaman bound for Bombay and the Bengal ports."

In 19th-cen. Liverpool, it was fashionable for wealthier women to wear white stockings. Each month on 'white stocking day', women went to the shipping office dressed in their best clothes to collect the 'allotment' which was the half pay of their sailing family member away at sea.

Possible interp.: verses are narrated by the sailor Riley, and choruses/refrains are his sweetheart at home?
